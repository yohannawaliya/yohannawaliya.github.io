Climatopia is a multilingual digital ecopoetry anthology project created by Yohanna Joseph Waliya in 2023 at the University of Calabar, Calabar-Nigeria. Climatopia as a term, it is coined from two words: climate and utopia, meaning the climatic perception of perfect world; as it is illustrated in the digital poetries accross the world. This digital ecopoetry anthology project selects e-works across the globe that sensitize the global community about the danger of climate change and suggested artistic and poetic ways to fight it in accoordance with diverse cultural belief systems as they correlate with African worldviews. We are still imploring e-poets to submit their best works to this global database. We are also sourcing works from mega databases and exhibiting them to the whole world.

  • Climatophosis 2.0

    Climatophosis 2.0 is the second version of Climatophosis. It is expressed in three languages-French, English and Margi. It is reconfigured with thunder rumbling sound as a metaphor of destruction. It is played freely on smartphones. Climatophosis 2.0 is inspired by the current climate change in the world along side the role of digital poetry in bettering social ecosystem. Its meaning is coined from climate and metamorphosis i.e. climate change. This climate crisis affects rainfall in the North-Eastern Nigeria, North-Central, and now North West where it snowed precisely in Zaria, Kaduna State, in the fourth quater of year 2022. For about two decades ago, it has been raining from May to October with little rainfall but now it’s changed from June to November with rainfall volumes increased to the extent that new variety of crops are needed to fit the new climate. Moreover, Climatophosis 2.0 is all about us, human beings and our envrionment. We are to be blamed for climate change. Our complex industrial activities don't respect nature. They release chlofluorocarbons (CFCs) into the atmosphire to deplete ozone layer. On the other hand, nature is renewing itself because it’s worn-out. This masterpieace is a call for masses to respect nature and be freed from the consequences of climate change. The consequences of climate change is obvious everywhere.   • Yohanna Joseph Waliya, a Nigerian digital ecopoet.

    Yohanna Joseph Waliya is a Nigerian digital poet, distant writer, novelist, playwright, winner of the Janusz Korczak Prize for Global South, Electronic Literature Organization Research Fellow and UNESCO Janusz Korczak Fellow. He writes in English and French. Among his works are : La révolte de vie (play), Monde 2.0 (play), Hégémonie Disparue (novel), Quand l'Afrique se lèvera (novel), Homosalus (digital poetry), Momenta (digital poetry), @TinyKorczak (Twitterbot-poetry), @KorzakD (Twitterbot playwright), Climatophosis (digital poetry: Winner of the best use Digital humanities tool for fun) etc. He is the Curator of MAELD & ADELD, Director of AELA & ADELI, a lecturer at the Department of Modern Languages and Translation Studies, University of Calabar as well as a postgraduate student at Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, Nigeria. His research interests cover distant writing, Digital Poetry, Twitterbot-poetry, Twitterature, language discourse etc.

Climatopie est un projet du florilège multilingue d'écopoésie numérique créé en 2023 par Yohanna Joseph Waliya,à l'Université de Calabar, Calabar-Nigéria. Climatopie en tant que terme, c'est inventé de mot-valise: climat et utopie, signifiant la perception du monde perfait sans changement climatique ; comme démontré dans les poésies numériques du monde entier. Ce florilège d'écopoésie numérique trie les meilleurs poemes animés du monde qui sensiblisent la comminauté globale du dangier de changement climatique ainsi que suggère les moyens artistiques et poétiques de combattre ce phinomène en tandem de la vision culturelle du monde vis-à-vis celle de l'Afrique. Nous sollicitons toujours aux poètes de nous remettre les meilleurs de leurs ouvrages sur le changement climatique pour que nous répertoriions dans cette base de données globale. Nous recensons également les grandes bases de données de poésie numérique pour trier ce genre d'ouvrage afin de les exposer.
